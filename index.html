What to Expect
Transit passes, pickpocket tips & local do's and don'ts for each city
▼
🏙️ Madrid Apr 29 – May 2

🚇 Metro Pass

  • Buy a Tarjeta Multi (€2.50) at any metro machine — English available. Load with Tourist Travel Pass: 1 day €10 · 2 days €17 · 3 days €22.50 · 5 days €32.50
  • Zone A covers all of central Madrid including airport — no extra airport fee with Tourist Pass
  • Each person needs their own card — not shareable. Buy at the airport on arrival or at Atocha station
→ Official Madrid Tourist Pass Info

🚨 Pickpocket Tips

  • Lower risk than Barcelona, but stay alert at Puerta del Sol, Gran Vía, Plaza Mayor and the Metro
  • Terrace scam: Someone puts a map on your café table to cover your phone — put your hand over it immediately
  • Rosemary scam: Never accept rosemary from strangers — it's a distraction tactic
  • Never leave phone or wallet on a café table or hanging on a chair back. Nothing in back pockets

✅ Do's & Don'ts

  • Do eat late — lunch 2–4 PM, dinner rarely before 9 PM
  • Do greet with "Buenos días" when entering shops or bars
  • Don't tip excessively — rounding up is standard; 10% is generous
  • Do carry some cash — smaller tapas bars often don't accept cards
🥘 Valencia May 2–5

🚇 Transit Pass

  • Buy a 10-trip card (~€9.30) at any metro station — shareable between two people
  • Single tickets ~€1.50 for occasional use. City center, beach and City of Arts are easily walkable
  • Taxis and Cabify/Uber are affordable and widely available

🚨 Pickpocket Tips

  • Valencia is notably safer than Barcelona or Madrid for petty theft
  • Still stay alert in the Central Market, El Carmen, and at bus/metro stations
  • Don't leave valuables unattended on the beach. Standard precautions apply

✅ Do's & Don'ts

  • Do try agua de Valencia — cava, orange juice & vodka cocktail
  • Do eat paella at lunch — locals never eat it for dinner
  • Don't put chorizo in paella — Valencians take this very seriously!
  • Do try horchata from a local horchatería — Valencia is its birthplace
🏖️ Barcelona May 5–8

🚇 Metro / Transit Pass

  • Barcelona Card (best option): unlimited metro/bus + free entry to 25+ museums. 3-day from ~€53
  • T-Casual 10-trip card ~€12.15 — shareable between two people · Single ticket ~€2.55
  • Barcelona Card may pay for itself quickly — Picasso Museum alone is €14 per person

🚨 Pickpocket Tips — IMPORTANT

  • Highest pickpocket concentration in Spain — stay vigilant on La Rambla and the Metro
  • Highest risk: La Rambla, La Boqueria, Metro L3, Gothic Quarter, Barceloneta beach, stations
  • Phone leash strongly recommended — phones snatched even from your hand
  • Crossbody bags worn in front — backpacks easily unzipped on the metro
  • Never put bag on back of a restaurant chair · Don't check phone map while walking
  • Leave passport in hotel safe — carry a photocopy · Avoid El Raval late at night

✅ Do's & Don'ts

  • Do say "Gràcies" not "Gracias" — locals appreciate the Catalan
  • Don't eat on La Rambla itself — overpriced; duck one street over
  • Do enjoy vermut (vermouth) hour around noon–2 PM on weekends
  • Do explore El Born, Gràcia and Poblenou — where locals actually hang out

Spanish Cuisine
Meal times, what things are called, specialty dishes & cuisines by city
▼
⏰ The Daily Rhythm of Eating

Spain runs 2–3 hours later than you're used to. Restaurants will be empty at 7 PM and packed at 10 PM. This is the culture — lean into it.

7–10 AM
Desayuno Breakfast
Light — coffee with tostada (toast rubbed with tomato and olive oil) or a croissant. Eaten standing at a bar.
9:30–11:30 AM
Almuerzo Mid-Morning · Valencia only 🥘
A uniquely Valencian tradition — a second breakfast of a bocadillo (sandwich) and coffee, mid-morning. Look for bars packed with locals around 10:30 AM.
2–4 PM
Comida Lunch — the main meal
The most important meal of the day. Multi-course, leisurely, often with wine. This is when you eat paella. Order the menú del día — typically €12–18 for 3 courses with bread and a drink. Don't arrive before 2 PM.
6–8 PM
Merienda Afternoon Snack
A light snack or coffee in the late afternoon — pastries, a small bite, a vermouth. Optional for visitors.
7–9 PM
Aperitivo / Vermut Pre-Dinner Drinks
The golden hour — vermouth, small beers (cañas), and light tapas. Nobody is eating a full dinner yet. Barcelona has a strong weekend vermut culture starting at noon.
9–11:30 PM
Cena Dinner
Dinner does not start before 9 PM. Restaurants before then are empty or serving confused tourists. 9:30 PM is perfectly normal. Stay late, order dessert.
🗺️ What to Eat Where
🏙️ Madrid Apr 29 – May 2
Castilian tradition meets modern creativity — Spain's culinary capital
🥚 Tortilla Española — Spain's most debated dish. Potato & egg omelette. Best in Spain at Casa Dani.
🐖 Cocido Madrileño — Madrid's signature chickpea stew with chorizo and meat. A slow-cooked Sunday tradition.
🐷 Jamón Ibérico — 100% acorn-fed bellota jamón is in another league. Order it everywhere.
🍞 Bocadillo de Calamares — Fried squid in a bread roll. A Madrid street-food icon near Plaza Mayor.
🍷 Vermouth Culture — Vermouth on tap is a Madrid tradition. Taberna de Angel Sierra in Chueca is the essential stop.
🥚 Callos Madrileños — Tripe stew with chorizo and morcilla. Classic taberna dish — deeply Madrid.
🏰 Toledo May 1 · Day Trip
Medieval Castilian cooking — game, stews, and the world's best marzipan
🍖 Carcamusas — Toledo's signature dish: tender pork slow-cooked with tomato and peas. Order at Taberna el Gallo.
🎂 Mazapán — Toledo is the marzipan capital of Spain. Santo Tomé is the most famous shop. Buy a box to take home.
🦌 Perdiz & Venado — Partridge and venison from the Castilian countryside. Often braised in wine.
🥘 Valencia May 2–5
The birthplace of paella — rice, citrus, seafood, and horchata
🥘 Paella Valenciana — Chicken, rabbit & beans — never chorizo, never seafood. Wood-fired, with socarrat (crispy bottom). Eaten at lunch only.
🦑 Fideuà — Paella's cousin made with short noodles instead of rice. Originated near Valencia.
🥛 Horchata de Chufa — Cold, creamy drink from tiger nuts. Valencia is its birthplace. Have it at Santa Catalina with fartons.
🍊 Agua de Valencia — Cava + fresh OJ + vodka + gin. Valencia's signature cocktail. Order a jarra (pitcher).
🐟 Clóchinas — Valencia's smaller, sweeter mussels. Best at Casa Montaña with house vermouth.
🐟 All'i Pebre — Spiced eel stew from the Albufera lagoon. Garlic, paprika, pimentón. A classic found nowhere else.
🏖️ Barcelona May 5–8
Catalan cuisine — distinct from Spanish, with French influence and seafood at its heart
🍅 Pa amb Tomàquet — Bread rubbed with raw tomato and olive oil. The foundation of Catalan eating. Served with almost everything.
💣 La Bomba — Fried potato ball stuffed with meat, served with spicy aioli. Invented at La Cova Fumada in Barceloneta.
🥚 Crema Catalana — The Catalan answer to crème brûlée — lighter, with citrus and cinnamon. Order it everywhere for dessert.
🐟 Esqueixada — Salt cod salad with tomato, olives, and onion. Refreshing and sharp — perfect in warm May.
🥂 Cava — Spain's sparkling wine, made just outside Barcelona. At El Xampanyet, house cava starts at €2 a glass.
🥩 Botifarra — Catalan pork sausage, grilled and served with white beans. Simple, perfect, deeply local.
🧭 Universal Rules
🕘 Never eat dinner before 9 PM. Restaurants will be empty and the experience suffers. The magic happens late.
💶 Always order the menú del día for lunch. It's the local way — eat a €50 meal for €14.
🍷 House wine (vino de la casa) is almost always excellent — €3–5 a glass. Don't overthink it.
💵 Carry some cash. Many old-school tabernas and bars are cash only.
🤝 Sharing is expected. Order raciones (full shared plates). Tapas are small tasters.
🫙 Tapas are not free here. Unlike Granada or Almería, in Madrid, Valencia and Barcelona you pay for what you order.

🌅 Old Town Route
El Carmen & Old City
Any afternoon · 3–7 PM · 4 stops · ~€25–40pp

🌙 Ruzafa Route
Ruzafa Neighborhood Crawl
Evening · 8 PM onward · 4 stops · ~€35–55pp

Barcelona
May 5–8
🌅 El Born Route
El Born — Steps from the Hotel
Any evening · 7 PM onward · 4 stops · ~€30–45pp
1Nømad Coffee — Barcelona's best specialty roasters. Walk over (2 min from hotel) for an afternoon coffee before the evening begins.
2El Xampanyet (Carrer de Montcada) — open since 1929, house cava from €2, anchovies, tortilla, the soul of El Born. Arrive at 7 PM sharp.
3Bar del Pla (El Born) — best patatas bravas in BCN, 30+ natural wines, oxtail, shirts that say "F**k Sangria, Drink Natural Wine"
4Bar Brutal (El Born) — grungy natural wine bar, loud and lively, late-night energy. Perfect final stop before heading home.
🌙 Barceloneta + Poble-sec
Beyond El Born — Venture Out
Daytime/evening · flexible · 4 stops · ~€30–50pp
1La Cova Fumada (Barceloneta) — birthplace of the bomba, cash only, closes when sold out, open 9 AM–3 PM. Go early.
2Quimet & Quimet (Poble-sec) — standing room only, montaditos with tinned seafood, house vermouth, midday only (noon–4 PM)
3Cal Pep (El Born) — Barcelona's most storied tapas counter. The tortilla española here is, by consensus, in a category of its own.
4Bodega La Puntual (Carrer de Montcada) — oyster bar at the entrance, Iberian arroz inside, excellent wine, Catalan charm
